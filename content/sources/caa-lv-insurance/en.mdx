Under EU Regulation (EU) No 785/2004 and Latvian law, all UAS operators are required to hold third-party liability insurance before conducting any flight operations.

## Who must hold insurance {#who}

Any natural or legal person operating a UAS in Latvia — including hobby pilots — must hold third-party liability (civil liability) insurance that covers the operation. The requirement applies regardless of UAS mass or category.

## Minimum coverage {#coverage}

The minimum coverage amount is set by Regulation (EU) No 785/2004 based on the maximum take-off mass (MTOM) of the UA:

| MTOM | Minimum coverage (SDR) |
|------|----------------------|
| ≤ 500 kg | 100,000 SDR |
| ≤ 1,000 kg | 150,000 SDR |
| ≤ 2,700 kg | 300,000 SDR |

For most hobby and commercial drones (< 25 kg), 100,000 SDR applies.

## How to obtain insurance {#how-to-obtain}

Insurance may be obtained from:
- Latvian insurance companies offering aviation/drone liability products
- Dedicated drone operator insurance providers (available online)

Some drone manufacturers or associations include liability insurance in membership packages.

## Official source

Current requirements and links to approved insurers are listed on the CAA Latvia website.
