# Open, Specific and Certified categories

EU Regulation 2019/947 splits all UAS operations **into three categories
by risk**.

## Open category

Lowest-risk operations — **no prior authorisation**. Constraints:

- VLOS;
- max 120 m altitude;
- max 25 kg mass;
- no flight over assemblies of people;
- no carriage of dangerous goods.

Subdivided into **A1, A2, A3** based on UAS class (C0–C4) and distance
to people.

## Specific category

Medium-risk operations — **requires authorisation or declaration**. E.g.,
BVLOS, flights above 120 m, flights over people with heavier UAS. The
operator runs a risk assessment (SORA) or uses a standard scenario (STS).

## Certified category

High-risk operations (passenger transport, large urban delivery). Requires
**both UAS certification and operator certification**. Out of scope for
MVP.

## For the A1/A3 exam

All MVP exam questions concern the **Open category**. Specific and
Certified are mentioned only conceptually — so the pilot knows that
levels exist above Open.

> **Source:** Reg (EU) 2019/947 Art. 3 and Annex; EASA Easy Access Rules
> for UAS (2024-07).
