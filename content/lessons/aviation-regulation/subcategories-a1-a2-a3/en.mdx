## The key principle

The UAS class marking (C0, C1, C2, C3, C4) is stamped on the drone's body. It is fixed by the manufacturer. **You cannot operate a C2 drone as if it were C0**, even if you would prefer the C0 rules.

## A1 — the lightest drones, nearest to people

A1 is for drones light enough that a collision causes minimal harm.

| Class | MTOM | Pilot certificate | Distance to individuals | Assemblies |
|-------|------|-------------------|------------------------|-----------|
| C0 | < 250 g | **None required** | Over individuals — OK | **Never** |
| C1 | < 900 g | A1/A3 online exam | No overflight expected | **Never** |

"No overflight expected" for C1 means: if you reasonably expect a person to be below your flight path, you must route around them or descend. It is a risk management obligation, not a physical distance rule.

## A2 — professionals working near people

A2 exists for commercial work where 30 m (or even 5 m) from people is operationally required. It demands more from both the drone and the pilot.

| Class | MTOM | Pilot certificate |
|-------|------|-------------------|
| C2 | < 4 kg | A1/A3 **plus** A2 competence certificate |

The A2 add-on: a further theory exam (60 Q, ≥75 %, at a recognised organisation in Latvia) plus a practical self-study declaration covering five skill areas.

Minimum horizontal distances for C2:

- **Low-speed mode active (≤ 3 m/s)** → 5 m from uninvolved persons
- **Normal speed** → 30 m from uninvolved persons

## A3 — away from people, away from settlements

A3 is for heavier drones operating in genuinely uninhabited areas.

| Class | MTOM | Pilot certificate |
|-------|------|-------------------|
| C2 / C3 / C4 | < 25 kg | A1/A3 online exam |
| Legacy (no marking) | < 25 kg | A1/A3 online exam |

Three limits apply simultaneously:
- ≥ 150 m from any residential, commercial, industrial, or recreational **area**
- ≥ 30 m from uninvolved **persons**, or the 1:1 rule (≥ flight altitude), whichever is greater
- ≥ a "2-second flight distance" at max speed (crash buffer)

<Scenario title="You own a DJI Mavic 3 Pro (895 g, marked C1). You want to fly commercially 10 m from a construction crew that has given consent. Which sub-category allows this?">
**None in Open category.** The Mavic is C1, which operates in A1. A1 requires no overflight of uninvolved persons. Construction crew who have consented might reduce the "uninvolved" argument, but 10 m close-approach flight is not within A1 rules.

For 10 m approach with consent, the operation would need an A2 certificate and a C2-marked drone — or a Specific category authorisation. The certificate you hold does not change the class of your drone.
</Scenario>

<CommonMistakes>
- Operating a C1 drone and expecting A1 to allow overflight of individuals — C1 in A1 does not allow overflight of uninvolved persons; only C0 does.
- Thinking the A1/A3 certificate authorises A2 operations — it does not; A2 needs the additional competence certificate.
- Confusing the 5 m A2 rule with a general "5 m is safe" — the 5 m only applies to C2 in confirmed low-speed mode.
- Applying A3's 150 m rule to individual buildings instead of the entire inhabited area.
- Legacy drone owners flying under A1 rules — a drone without class marking flies under A3 (or A1 if < 250 g), not A1 with C1 privileges.
</CommonMistakes>
