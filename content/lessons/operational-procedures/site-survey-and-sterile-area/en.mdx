## The site survey question set

Before powering up, walk the site and answer five questions:

1. **Where is the safest launch and landing point?**
2. **Where are people likely to move unexpectedly?**
3. **What obstacles affect departure, return, or emergency landing?**
4. **Where will the pilot stand to keep VLOS and space from others?**
5. **What will interrupt the sterile area if something changes?**

The point is to shape the environment before the aircraft adds time pressure.

## What “sterile area” means

A **sterile operating area** is a deliberate no-distraction zone during critical phases:

- takeoff,
- low-altitude manoeuvring near the launch point,
- approach,
- landing,
- battery swap if people are moving around the aircraft.

In that zone:

- non-essential conversation stops;
- clients and bystanders stay outside the boundary;
- the pilot is not answering creative requests or changing mission goals;
- the observer speaks only for safety-relevant issues.

## Why this matters

Takeoff and landing give the pilot the least time margin. A small distraction there causes larger consequences than the same distraction at safe altitude in stable cruise.

## Practical markers

The sterile area does not need expensive equipment. It can be built with:

- cones,
- a landing mat,
- a spoken briefing,
- a clear standing position for the client or observer,
- one sentence repeated before startup: **“Do not enter this area until I say landing is complete.”**

<Scenario title="You arrive for a property shoot. The client wants to stand beside you during takeoff so they can comment on framing immediately. The lawn is narrow and there is a decorative tree behind the launch spot. What is the correct setup?">
Move the launch point if needed. Build a simple sterile area first.

The pilot needs a clear takeoff and return path, distance from the tree, and a boundary that keeps the client outside the critical zone until the aircraft is stable. The right workflow is:

1. choose the safest launch point, not the most convenient one;
2. place the client outside the sterile area;
3. brief them that framing discussion starts only after the aircraft is safely established;
4. keep takeoff and landing free from non-essential conversation.
</Scenario>

- Treating site survey as a quick look instead of a structured scan.
- Allowing mission discussion to continue during final approach.
