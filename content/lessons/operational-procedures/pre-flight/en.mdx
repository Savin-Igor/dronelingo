# Pre-flight check

An Open-category pilot must **inspect the site and the UAS before every
flight**. It is not a formal document — it is a few minutes of discipline
that separates professionals from incidents.

## Environment

- **Weather:** wind direction and speed (within UAS spec); visibility
  good enough to maintain VLOS; no rain / snow.
- **NOTAM and UGZ:** check `airspace.lv/drones`. If the zone is
  restricted — there must be an approved BGKIS application.
- **Surroundings:** identify obstacles (power lines, trees, pedestrians);
  visualise the planned trajectory.

## UAS

- **Software:** current firmware, valid geo-awareness dataset (ED-269).
- **Batteries:** charged, not swollen, temperature within spec.
- **Mechanics:** motors, propellers, frame free of cracks.
- **Sensors:** GPS has enough satellites, compass calibrated, IMU shows
  no warnings.
- **Safety setup:** Return-To-Home point and altitude defined, geofence
  active.

## Pilot fitness

IMSAFE self-check (see **Human performance → IMSAFE**). If not fit —
do not fly.

> **Source:** Reg (EU) 2019/947 Art. UAS.OPEN.060(1); EASA Easy Access
> Rules — Operational procedures.
