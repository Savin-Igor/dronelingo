# Emergency procedures

<MissionBriefing objective="After this lesson you will know the correct immediate actions for lost link, lost GPS, low battery, fly-away, and collision — and why these decisions must be made before takeoff, not during the emergency.">

## Lost link — the most common emergency

A lost control link means the drone can no longer receive commands from the pilot. The UAS should automatically execute the pre-configured **fail-safe**.

Two common fail-safe modes:
- **Return-to-Home (RTH)** — the drone navigates back to the home point at a pre-set altitude. The RTH altitude must be set higher than every obstacle on the return path before takeoff.
- **Controlled descent** — the drone descends and lands at its current position.

Choose the appropriate mode before takeoff. RTH is dangerous if the drone is above a crowd and the home point is in a different location. Descent is dangerous if the drone is over water or an obstacle.

## Lost GPS — manual is the only option

Without GPS, the drone cannot hold position or navigate autonomously. **Switch to manual mode immediately.** Use visual references (roads, buildings, field edges) to orient the drone and select a safe landing spot. Land as quickly as safely possible.

Do not attempt to wait for GPS recovery — position drift in altitude-hold mode without GPS will take the drone away from where you expect it.

## Low battery — two-stage response

Most drones warn at approximately 20 % remaining (first warning) and 10 % (second, critical warning).

**First warning (~20 %):** Abort the current mission objective. Start the return. Do not complete the shot, the pass, or the sequence.

**Second warning (~10 %):** Land immediately at the nearest safe spot. Do not try to reach the home point if the battery cannot make it — a controlled landing off-target is safer than a crash near the home point.

<Scenario title="You are filming 600 m from the launch point. The first battery warning appears. Your planned footage would take another 4 minutes. What do you do?">
**Start returning immediately.** A 600 m return at a moderate speed may take 3–4 minutes. Adding 4 minutes of filming first would result in the second warning triggering mid-return — and potentially a crash.

The professional action: abort, return, land, swap battery, resume shooting. Footage can always be re-filmed. A crashed drone in a field 400 m away cannot always be recovered the same day.
</Scenario>

## Fly-away — rare but serious

A fly-away occurs when the drone departs in an uncontrolled direction and cannot be recovered by normal control inputs. Causes include firmware bugs, GPS spoofing, or compass errors in extreme magnetic environments.

**Action sequence:**
1. Attempt to switch flight modes (e.g., to Attitude mode) to regain partial control.
2. If the drone cannot be recovered, note the last known GPS position (screenshot the app).
3. Track the drone as long as VLOS allows.
4. If a collision with infrastructure or manned aircraft is possible, call the nearest airport (if in controlled airspace).
5. File a mandatory report within 72 hours via e.caa.gov.lv/incidents.

## Collision and post-incident

With a **manned aircraft**: mandatory incident report within 72 hours at e.caa.gov.lv/incidents. This is not optional.

With an **obstacle or surface**: abort further flight, physically inspect the UAS before any subsequent flight. Document the event (photos, video, timestamp). File a voluntary report if the event had safety implications.

<CommonMistakes>
- Not setting RTH altitude before takeoff — default altitude may be lower than nearby obstacles.
- Continuing to film after the first battery warning — the second warning may occur over water or a crowd.
- Switching to manual mode without visual references available — without references, manual mode in GPS loss makes disorientation worse.
- Not filing a mandatory report after a collision with a manned aircraft — omission is a separate offence.
- Relying on the manufacturer app's "find my drone" function without knowing the last GPS screenshot — the app requires connectivity to retrieve the position.
</CommonMistakes>
