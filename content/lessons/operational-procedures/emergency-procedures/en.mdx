# Emergency procedures

Open-category flights are conducted without ATC support. Every
emergency is the pilot's call, based on procedures rehearsed in
advance.

## Lost link

Loss of the control link. The UAS executes a **fail-safe**:

- **Return-to-Home (RTH)** — flies back to a pre-set point;
- **Controlled descent** — lands directly under the last known
  position.

Choose the mode **before** flight. RTH altitude must clear every
obstacle along the trajectory.

## Lost GPS

Without GPS the UAS does not know its position. Switch to **manual
mode** and land at the nearest safe spot with visual references.

## Low battery

First warning (usually ~20 %): **abort the mission**, start return.
Second warning: **land immediately at a safe spot** — do not chase the
home point if the battery cannot make it.

## Fly-away

The UAS departs in an uncontrolled direction. Try to re-establish
control; if it fails, **document the last known position**
(photo / screenshot) and report to CAA Latvia within 72 hours if
manned aircraft or infrastructure were involved.

## Collision

- With a manned aircraft — **mandatory report** within 72 hours via
  `e.caa.gov.lv/incidents`.
- With an obstacle — abort, inspect the UAS, document the event.

> **Source:** EASA Easy Access Rules for UAS — Emergency procedures;
> manufacturer manuals (DJI / Autel / Skydio).
