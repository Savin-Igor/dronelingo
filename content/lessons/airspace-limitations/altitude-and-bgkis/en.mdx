# Altitude limits and BGKIS applications

The Open-category altitude cap is **120 m above ground or water**. A
UGZ can lower it — but **never raise it**.

## How altitude is measured

- **AGL (Above Ground Level)** — above the ground directly under the
  UAS, not above the launch point.
- Terrain following is the pilot's responsibility when there is a
  rise underneath.

## When to file BGKIS

Since 2025-01-01 a BGKIS application is mandatory for flights in a
**restricted UGZ**. File at least **24 hours in advance** unless a
shorter window is specified.

Application fields:

- date and time of flight (± buffer);
- max altitude and distance from launch point;
- UAS class and mass;
- pilot and operator numbers.

## When BGKIS alone is not enough

Some zones (airport CTRs, military sites) require **additional
authorisation** from the relevant authority. BGKIS does not replace
it.

## Consequences

Flying without an approved application — administrative fine and,
depending on the zone, criminal liability (e.g. flying within
EVR17 Border East along the Latvia-Russia border).

> **Source:** Reg (EU) 2019/947 UAS.OPEN.010(2); CAA Latvia BGKIS
> rules effective 2025-01-01.
