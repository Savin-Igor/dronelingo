## The 120 m ceiling

The Open category hard limit is **120 m above ground or water directly under the drone** — not above the takeoff point, not above sea level.

This has one critical consequence: **the ceiling moves with the terrain.**

A drone launched at the base of a 60 m hill must stay below 120 m above the ground where it flies. If it crosses to the top of the hill, the 120 m is measured from the hilltop — not from where it launched.

A UGZ can lower the ceiling for a specific area. It can **never raise it above 120 m**.

## When BGKIS is mandatory

Since 1 January 2025, a BGKIS application is required for any flight in a **Restricted UGZ**.

The application must include the date and time (with a buffer), maximum altitude, UAS class, mass, pilot number, and operator number.

## When BGKIS is not enough

Some zones require additional authorisation on top of a BGKIS submission — particularly airport CTRs and military restricted areas. BGKIS confirms that the zone manager has received your application; it does not grant access by itself.

<Scenario title="You want to film a hilltop castle. The castle sits at 180 m elevation; you launch from the valley floor at 120 m. The zone is green (information). How high can you fly from your launch point?">
You need to think in AGL above the terrain the drone flies over — not above your launch point.

The valley floor is at 120 m. The castle hilltop is at 180 m, a rise of 60 m. When you fly over the castle hill, your ceiling is 120 m above the hilltop — which is 180 + 120 = 300 m above sea level, but only 120 m above the ground under the drone. From your launch point (120 m elevation), the ceiling would appear as 180 m of climb available when you're over the valley, but only 120 m once you're over the hill.

In plain terms: do not exceed 120 m above whatever terrain is directly below you at any point.
</Scenario>

<CommonMistakes>
- Measuring 120 m from the takeoff point instead of from the ground currently under the UAS.
- Assuming that a green zone means no check is needed — always open airspace.lv before flying.
- Treating BGKIS submission as approval — a filed application is not the same as an authorised application.
- Flying in a restricted zone without a confirmed BGKIS response, assuming the 24 h window is just a guideline.
- Not updating RTH altitude when the terrain around the return path is higher than when you set it.
</CommonMistakes>
