## What is BGKIS and why it became mandatory

**BGKIS** (Bāzes ģeotelpiskās informācijas kontroļu un informēšanas par situāciju sistēma — Base Geospatial Control and Situational Awareness System) is the CAA Latvia portal at [e.caa.gov.lv](https://e.caa.gov.lv) through which pilots submit flight notifications for restricted UAS Geographical Zones (UGZ).

Before 1 January 2025 notifications were voluntary in most zones. Cabinet Regulation No. 248 (§53, §73) made them **mandatory**: any flight in a restricted UGZ requires a submitted and approved BGKIS notification before takeoff.

A denied or unanswered notification means no legal flight — not even a short hover.

## Step 1: Check the zone on airspace.lv/drones

Open **airspace.lv/drones** and navigate to your planned flight location. The map shows every UAS Geographical Zone in Latvia.

Click any zone to open its info panel. Record:
- **Zone identifier** (e.g. EVR-D-017)
- **Zone type** (Restricted, Conditional, Prohibited)
- **Zone manager** (CAA Latvia, airport, military, municipality)
- **Altitude limits** (lower and upper bounds)
- **Active hours** (permanent or time-window based)

## Step 2: Determine whether a notification is needed

| Zone type | Required action |
|-----------|----------------|
| No restriction (green) | No notification needed — fly within Open-category rules |
| Conditional (amber) | Submit BGKIS notification and wait for approval |
| Prohibited (red) | Do not fly in Open category — no notification possible |
| CTR / airport (grey) | Submit BGKIS notification and wait for approval |

**EVR17 Border East** — the zone along the Latvia-Russia and Latvia-Belarus border — is permanently closed to Open-category flights since 2025. No notification will be approved.

## Step 3: Submit the BGKIS notification

Log in to **e.caa.gov.lv** with your operator registration credentials. Navigate to **BGKIS → New notification**.

Fill in the form:

1. **Date and time window** — exact start and end time. Allow enough time for approval (same-day approvals are possible but not guaranteed; plan 24 h ahead for non-urgent flights).
2. **Location** — enter GPS coordinates or draw the flight polygon on the embedded map. Define the maximum altitude (AGL).
3. **UAS registration number** — your operator registration ID from CAA Latvia.
4. **Purpose** — brief description (commercial photography, survey, hobby, inspection, etc.).
5. **Emergency contact** — your phone number during the flight.

Submit the form. You will receive a reference number immediately.

## Step 4: Wait for approval — do not take off without it

Approval is issued by the zone manager (CAA Latvia, airport authority, military, etc.). Check the BGKIS portal for status:

- **Pending** — wait; do not take off.
- **Approved** — take off within the approved time window only.
- **Denied** — do not fly. If denied without explanation, contact the zone manager.

An approval for Tuesday 14:00–16:00 does **not** carry over to Wednesday. Each new flight window requires a new notification.

## Step 5: Fly within the approved window

During the flight:
- Stay within the geographic boundary and altitude stated in the notification.
- Have the approval reference number accessible (screenshot or print) — an inspector may request it.
- If you need to extend the time window, submit a new notification before the old one expires.

<Scenario title="You check airspace.lv and see your planned filming location sits inside an amber zone managed by CAA Latvia. You plan to fly tomorrow at 10:00. What do you do today?">
**Submit the BGKIS notification today.** Log in to e.caa.gov.lv, create a notification for tomorrow's time window (e.g. 09:45–11:15 to include setup time), define the flight polygon and maximum altitude, and submit.

Do not wait until tomorrow morning — same-day approvals are not guaranteed. If the notification is still pending at 09:45 tomorrow, you cannot legally take off. The professional approach: submit 24 hours ahead and monitor the portal for status.
</Scenario>

<CommonMistakes>
- Submitting the notification and taking off before approval arrives — pending is not the same as approved.
- Not accounting for setup time in the notification window — if the flight is 10:00–11:00, the window should start earlier to allow equipment setup.
- Relying on the manufacturer app to determine if a notification is needed — airspace.lv is the only legally binding source.
- Thinking a denied notification can be appealed in time for the same flight — plan ahead to avoid last-minute denials.
- Not checking NOTAM on the same day as the flight, even after receiving BGKIS approval — a temporary restriction can override the zone status.
</CommonMistakes>
