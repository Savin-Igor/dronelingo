## The only legally binding source

Before every flight, open **airspace.lv/drones**. This is not optional and it is not a formality — it is the only map that carries legal force in Latvia. Commercial apps, Google Maps, and forum screenshots are helpful, but they are not the law.

## Three zone types

**Information zones** — no restrictions apply. The zone exists only to warn you that something is nearby (a bird nesting area, a military practice range that is currently inactive). Flying is allowed without any application.

**Restricted zones** — flight is allowed under specific conditions, which the zone definition spells out. Conditions can include a BGKIS application, a maximum altitude lower than 120 m, a specific time window, or all of the above. Since 2025-01-01, a BGKIS application is mandatory for any restricted zone that requires it.

**Prohibited zones** — flights are not permitted at all for Open category. Includes airport CTRs (control zones), military sites actively in use, and the border zones.

## What authorisation does not give you

Getting BGKIS approval for a restricted zone does not suspend the other Open-category rules. You still must:

- stay below 120 m AGL;
- maintain VLOS;
- never fly over assemblies of people.

The zone access is expanded. The category limits are not.

<Scenario title="Your BGKIS application for a restricted area near Riga airport is approved. The approval says 'max 70 m AGL'. Can you fly up to 120 m inside the restricted area?">
**No.** The BGKIS approval sets a maximum of 70 m AGL for this specific authorisation. That is lower than the general Open-category 120 m ceiling — and the lower limit applies. The zone conditions always take precedence when they are more restrictive.
</Scenario>

## Latvia's closed border zone

Since 2025, the **EVR17 Border East** zone runs the entire length of the Latvia–Russia and Latvia–Belarus borders. It is classified as prohibited for Open-category flights. Flying in this zone without a specific military or government authorisation carries criminal liability — not just an administrative fine.

<CommonMistakes>
- Checking the map once at the start of the season and not again before each individual flight — zones change.
- Confusing "information" with "restricted" — they look similar on some map views; check the legend.
- Assuming a BGKIS-approved restricted zone allows 120 m — the approval may set a lower ceiling.
- Flying near the eastern border without verifying the exact EVR17 boundary on the current map.
- Believing that a competitor's drone flying in an area means it must be allowed — someone else's violation is not your authorisation.
</CommonMistakes>
