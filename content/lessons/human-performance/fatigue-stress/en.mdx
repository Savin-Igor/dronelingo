# Fatigue and stress for the UAS pilot

A remote pilot works alone — no second pilot, no ATC. That makes
**personal state** one of the dominant safety factors.

## What "fatigue" really is

After 16 hours awake, reaction time and attention span degrade
similarly to a 0.5 ‰ blood-alcohol concentration. After 24 hours —
similar to 1.0 ‰.

## Sources of stress

- **Acute stress** — time pressure, a waiting client, hardware
  glitches;
- **Chronic stress** — week-long workload, family circumstances;
- **Technical stress** — repeated BGKIS rejections, GPS spoofing near
  the border.

## How to know your limit

Before flight, ask three plain questions:

1. Can I make a **safe decision** under time pressure?
2. Is my attention on multiple things or on **this flight**?
3. Am I ready to **abort the mission** if things go sideways?

If any answer is "no" — postpone or delegate.

## Practical tips

- Schedule flights **after rest**, not after a long workday.
- **Water, not coffee** as the primary fluid before flight.
- **Cap sessions at 60–90 min.** Then break.
- **File voluntary occurrence reports** so the lessons stick.

> **Source:** ICAO Doc 9966 — Manual for Fatigue Management;
> EASA Easy Access Rules for UAS — Human Performance Limitations.
