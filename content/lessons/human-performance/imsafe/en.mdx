# IMSAFE self-check before flight

In any UAS operation the human is the weakest link. Before every flight
the pilot runs the **IMSAFE** checklist to confirm fitness to fly.

## Six checks

| Letter | Topic | Question to self |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| **I**llness | Illness | Cold, fever, flu-like symptoms? |
| **M**edication | Medication | Anything that affects reaction time? |
| **S**tress | Stress | Emotionally loaded (work, family)? |
| **A**lcohol | Alcohol | Anything within the last 8 hours? |
| **F**atigue | Fatigue | How much sleep last night? |
| **E**ating | Eating | Eaten within the last 4 hours? |

## Why it matters for a UAS pilot

An Open-category pilot works alone. **Nobody else notices** that the
pilot is tired or distracted today. IMSAFE is a personal discipline —
the responsibility lies entirely with the pilot.

## Practical limit

If **even one answer is "yes"**, the flight should be postponed or
delegated to an observer. It is far cheaper to slip a flight by a few
hours than to live with an incident that follows the pilot for life.

> **Source:** EASA Easy Access Rules for UAS — Human Performance
> Limitations; ICAO Doc 9683 (Human Factors Training Manual).
