# Occurrence reporting

Civil aviation safety relies on **open reporting of occurrences** — even
those that ended without consequences. That is how the industry learns
from near-misses rather than only from accidents.

## Mandatory within 72 hours

The pilot reports **within 72 hours** if:

- there was a **serious injury or fatality**;
- there was a **near-miss or collision with a manned aircraft**;
- the UAS struck an obstacle that is critical infrastructure (power
  line, railway).

## Voluntary reporting

Same channel, no deadline, **for any safety-related event**:

- lost data link;
- "fly-away" — UAS departs in an uncontrolled direction;
- "fail-safe" did not activate;
- erroneous geofence or GPS position.

## Where to file

- **Latvia:** [e.caa.gov.lv/incidents](https://e.caa.gov.lv/incidents).
- **EU-wide:** [aviationreporting.eu](https://aviationreporting.eu) —
  ECCAIRS system.

## "Just culture"

EU law (Reg 376/2014) protects the reporter from criminal consequences
unless the event resulted from deliberate misconduct. Reporting is
safer than silence.

> **Source:** Reg (EU) 376/2014 on the reporting of occurrences in
> civil aviation; CAA Latvia portal `e.caa.gov.lv/incidents`.
