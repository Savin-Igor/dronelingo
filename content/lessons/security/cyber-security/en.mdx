# UAS cyber security

A UAS is a connected computer system — pilot, drone, mobile app,
manufacturer cloud. Each is a potential attack vector.

## Basic hygiene

- **Change the factory password** on the mobile app and account.
- **Update firmware** only from the official manufacturer channel.
- **Do not connect the UAS to unknown Wi-Fi** (public hotspots, forum
  "demo" networks).
- **Store account passwords** in a password manager, not in plain
  text.

## GNSS jamming and spoofing

- **Jamming** — RF noise drowns out the GPS signal; the UAS loses
  position lock. Sign: sudden RTH failure or a typical "grey map" in
  city centres.
- **Spoofing** — fake GPS signals redirect the UAS. Sign: position
  jumps to an implausible spot (kilometres off).

Latvia is seeing more such events, especially near the eastern border.
**First action:** switch to manual mode, land safely.
**Second action:** report to CAA Latvia.

## Data privacy on the UAS

A camera-equipped UAS **stores video locally on SD or in the cloud**.
Make sure:

- the SD card is encrypted if the content is sensitive;
- the cloud account is protected with 2FA;
- videos are deleted after the required retention window.

## Occurrence reporting

If a credible attack on the account or UAS firmware is suspected, it
is a **voluntary report** at `e.caa.gov.lv/incidents`.

> **Source:** ENISA Good Practices for UAS cyber security (2022);
> ENISA threat report — GNSS spoofing/jamming.
