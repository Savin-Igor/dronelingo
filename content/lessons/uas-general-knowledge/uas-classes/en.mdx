# UAS classes C0–C4

## What class markings are

EU Regulation 2019/945 defines five UAS class markings — C0 through C4. Each is physically stamped on the drone body with a CE mark. The marking determines which Open-category sub-category the drone may be used in. **You cannot re-classify a drone** by choosing to operate under different rules.

## The five classes

| Class | MTOM | Key requirement | Sub-category |
|-------|------|-----------------|--------------|
| **C0** | < 250 g, max 19 m/s | No marking needed on small toys | A1 only |
| **C1** | < 900 g | Remote ID, geo-awareness | A1 only |
| **C2** | < 4 kg | Low-speed mode (≤ 3 m/s) | A2 or A3 |
| **C3** | < 25 kg | Remote ID, geo-awareness | A3 only |
| **C4** | < 25 kg | Manual control only (no autonomous modes) | A3 only |

**C0** is the only class that permits overflying uninvolved individuals — but **never** assemblies of people. **C1** in A1 requires the pilot to plan around people, not over them.

## Legacy drones — no class marking

Drones manufactured before the class system took effect may not carry any class marking. These "legacy" UAS are slotted by mass:

- **< 250 g** — treated as C0, may operate in A1.
- **250 g to 25 kg** — treated as C3, must operate in A3.

A legacy drone that weighs 800 g gets **A3 rules** — not A1 — even though a new C1 drone at the same weight operates in A1. The certification matters, not just the mass.

## Why C2 is the professional pivot

C2 is the only class that enables **A2 operations** — close commercial work within 5–30 m of uninvolved persons. The pilot must hold the A1/A3 certificate **plus** the A2 competence certificate. A C2 drone flown by a pilot with only the A1/A3 certificate must still follow A3 rules: 150 m from inhabited areas, 30 m from persons.

<Scenario title="You hold an A1/A3 certificate and your drone is marked C2. A client asks you to film 25 m from a group of construction workers who have not given consent. Can you do it?">
**No.** With only the A1/A3 certificate, a C2 drone must operate in A3. A3 requires ≥ 30 m from uninvolved persons. 25 m violates that limit.

To close to 25 m, you would need the A2 competence certificate. The construction workers' professional presence does not automatically make them "involved persons" under the regulation — consent alone is insufficient; the correct sub-category authorisation is also required.
</Scenario>

<CommonMistakes>
- Assuming a heavy C1 drone (e.g. 850 g) can fly closer to people than a light C2 — C1 drones never operate under A2 rules regardless of mass.
- Flying a C2 drone in A2 with only the A1/A3 certificate — the A2 competence certificate is a separate requirement.
- Thinking a legacy drone can claim A1 privileges by mass — a 300 g legacy drone is A3, not A1.
- Confusing C4 "manual control only" with "no certificate needed" — C4 still requires the A1/A3 certificate for A3 operations.
- Believing the pilot can "downgrade" a C2 to operate it under C0 rules — class is fixed by manufacture.
</CommonMistakes>
