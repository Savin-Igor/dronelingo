# UAS classes C0–C4

EU Regulation 2019/945 defines **class markings** that determine which
Open-category sub-category a UAS may operate in.

## Five markings

| Class | MTOM | Typical use |
|-------|------|-------------|
| **C0** | < 250 g, max speed 19 m/s | A1 — overflight of individuals allowed (not assemblies). |
| **C1** | < 900 g | A1 — reasonably expected not to fly over uninvolved people. |
| **C2** | < 4 kg, with low-speed mode | A2 — min. 5 m horizontal (low-speed) or 30 m (normal). |
| **C3** | < 25 kg | A3 — at least 150 m from residential / commercial areas. |
| **C4** | < 25 kg, manual control only | A3 — model-aircraft style operations. |

## Where to find the class

The class marking is **on the UAS body** (CE mark + class, e.g., "C1").
Older drones (pre-2024) may not have a marking — those are called "legacy"
and are placed by mass.

## Legacy drones

If the drone is not marked:

- < 250 g — A1 as C0;
- < 25 kg — A3 as C3.

## Why it matters

**You cannot fly a C2 or C3 drone in A1** — regardless of pilot skill.
The class fixes the sub-category, not the pilot's choice.

> **Source:** Reg (EU) 2019/945 Annex (C0–C4 requirements); EASA Easy
> Access Rules for UAS — Subcategories.
