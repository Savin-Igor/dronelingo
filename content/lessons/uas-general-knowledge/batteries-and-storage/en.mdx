# Batteries and storage

The battery is both the **energy source** and the **biggest safety
risk** of a UAS. Most "drone fire" incidents trace back to a damaged
or mis-stored Li-Po cell.

## Li-Po basics

- **Voltage range:** 3.0–4.2 V per cell (safe working range
  ~3.7–4.2 V).
- **Energy:** Wh = Ah × V. E.g., 4S 5000 mAh ≈ 14.8 V × 5 Ah ≈ **74 Wh**.
- **C rating:** the maximum discharge current as a multiple of
  capacity.

## Storage

| Storage period | Recommended charge |
|----------------|--------------------|
| < 3 days | 100 % (if you'll use it soon) |
| 3–14 days | 70–80 % |
| **> 2 weeks** | **50–60 % (storage charge)** |

Keep cells in a **fire-safe bag** (Li-Po safe bag) and in a **cool,
dry place**.

## If a battery swells

**Do not use it.** Swelling means internal gas build-up — fire risk.
Dispose of it under local rules (in Latvia via partner collection
points listed at `dabasdati.lv`).

## Air travel

IATA rules:

- **< 100 Wh** — checked or carry-on baggage (no charging in flight);
- **100–160 Wh** — carry-on only, with airline approval;
- **> 160 Wh** — **forbidden** as civilian air cargo.

> **Source:** Reg (EU) 2019/945 on UAS battery safety; IATA Lithium
> Battery Guidance Document; manufacturer manuals.
