# Optional insurance covers

<MissionBriefing objective="After this lesson you will select the correct insurance stack for a given operating scenario — recreational, commercial photography, inspection, or EU travel — and understand what each optional policy covers that the mandatory one does not.">
The mandatory third-party policy covers what you do to others. Optional covers protect your equipment, your income, and your professional reputation. A professional operator needs all three layers.
</MissionBriefing>

## Hull insurance — protecting the aircraft

Hull insurance covers **damage to or loss of the UAS itself**. The mandatory policy never covers this. Hull insurance typically covers:

- **Crash** — regardless of cause: pilot error, fly-away, unexpected obstacle, bird strike.
- **Theft** — from a vehicle, storage location, or on-site.
- **Water damage** — if the drone is rated for wet conditions and the damage exceeds that rating.
- **Transit damage** — in some policies, damage during transport.

**Typical cost:** 5–10 % of UAS replacement value per year, with a deductible of €200–500 per claim.

A DJI Mavic 3 Enterprise priced at €3 500 would cost approximately €175–350/year to insure on hull. Without hull cover, a fly-away over water is an unrecovered €3 500 loss.

## Professional liability — protecting the work

For commercial operators, **professional liability** (also called errors and omissions insurance) covers:

- **Client financial losses** if the deliverable fails — footage corrupted, survey data lost, deadline missed due to equipment failure.
- **Data loss** — GIS survey results, orthomosaic data, inspection records.
- **IP and copyright claims** — if footage captures protected content inadvertently.

Without professional liability, a client who loses a major construction survey due to a data corruption event can pursue the operator for the full cost of re-survey — which may exceed the value of the drone by orders of magnitude.

## Payload insurance — protecting the camera

Sensors, cinema cameras, and LiDAR modules mounted on a drone often cost more than the aircraft itself. Payload insurance covers this equipment separately, including:

- Physical damage during flight or landing.
- Damage during transit and setup.
- Theft from a vehicle or location.

A Sony FX3 rigged for aerial work plus gimbal can exceed €5 000. A hull policy covers the drone frame; the camera requires its own rider or separate policy.

## EU travel policy

The mandatory Latvian policy is **valid across the EU** for third-party liability — EU regulations standardise this. However, for extended commercial work in other member states, an **EU travel extension** is advisable because:

- Some countries (notably Germany) impose additional requirements on commercial UAS operators beyond EU minimums.
- Local claims handling in a foreign country is smoother with a policy that covers the jurisdiction explicitly.
- Long-term operations abroad may trigger local operator registration requirements.

<Scenario title="You are a commercial aerial photographer. During a client job, a fly-away sends your drone into a canal. The drone, an attached cinema camera, and the day's footage are all lost. What does each policy cover?">
- **Mandatory third-party liability:** Covers any property or people the drone struck during the fly-away. Does not cover your losses.
- **Hull insurance:** Covers the drone replacement cost (subject to deductible).
- **Payload insurance:** Covers the cinema camera replacement cost.
- **Professional liability:** May cover the client's losses if the lost footage was a contractual deliverable and the failure is attributable to your operations.

Without the optional layers, you absorb the cost of the drone and camera out of pocket, and face a client claim with no insurance backing.
</Scenario>

<CommonMistakes>
- Assuming hull cover is included in the mandatory policy — it is not, ever.
- Not insuring expensive payload separately — the hull policy insures the aircraft, not the sensor.
- Believing the EU policy covers all EU commercial requirements — Germany and a few others impose additional demands worth checking per job.
- Letting professional liability lapse between contracts — a claim from a job six months ago can still arrive after the policy expires if the coverage period is misunderstood.
- Underinsuring hull by using a lower declared value to reduce premiums — the payout on a total loss is based on the declared value.
</CommonMistakes>
