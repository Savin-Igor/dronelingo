# Latvia mandatory insurance minima

<MissionBriefing objective="After this lesson you will know which UAS operators in Latvia must hold third-party liability insurance, the minimum cover amounts by drone class, and what the policy must actually cover — and what it explicitly does not.">
Insurance is not optional for most UAS operators in Latvia. The requirement exists regardless of whether the flight is commercial or recreational. No policy, no legal flight.
</MissionBriefing>

## Who must hold insurance

Cabinet Regulation No. 447 requires **all UAS operators in Latvia** to hold valid third-party liability insurance before flying — with one exception:

- **C0 drones under 250 g** — no mandatory insurance requirement.
- **All other classes** — mandatory, regardless of recreational or commercial use.

This is a stricter requirement than many other EU member states. A recreational pilot flying a DJI Mini 4 Pro (249 g, C0) is exempt. The same pilot flying a 300 g drone is not.

## Minimum cover amounts

| Drone class / mass | Minimum third-party cover |
|-------------------|--------------------------|
| C0 (< 250 g) | **Not required** |
| A1 (C1), A2, A3 — drones up to 20 kg | **€50 000** |
| A3 — C3 / C4 drones (20–25 kg) | **750 000 SDR** |
| Specific category (UAS < 500 kg) | **750 000 SDR** |

**SDR** = Special Drawing Rights (IMF currency basket). At current rates, 750 000 SDR ≈ €900 000. The exact euro equivalent fluctuates — check the Bank of Latvia rate when purchasing a policy.

## What the mandatory cover protects

The mandatory policy covers **bodily injury and property damage caused to third parties** during a UAS operation. It is liability insurance, not asset insurance.

**What it does not cover:**
- Damage to the UAS itself (the drone, camera, payload) — this requires **hull insurance**.
- Damage to property the pilot or operator owns.
- Losses from a client contract not being fulfilled — this requires **professional liability** insurance.
- Theft of the UAS.

Understanding what is excluded prevents the common mistake of assuming the mandatory policy provides full financial protection.

## Proof of insurance

The insurance certificate must be:
- **Available on-site** during every flight.
- **Presented on request** to any CAA Latvia inspector.
- **Current** — expired policies are treated as no policy.

An operator flying without a valid policy receives an **administrative fine** and may have the operation halted. The fine does not substitute for the insurance obligation.

<Scenario title="A recreational pilot owns a DJI Air 3 (720 g, marked C1). They fly only on weekends over their own fields, never commercially, never near people. Do they need insurance?">
**Yes.** The exemption applies only to C0 drones under 250 g. The DJI Air 3 is a C1 drone. Cabinet Regulation No. 447 requires third-party liability insurance regardless of recreational use, regardless of flying over own land, regardless of how rarely the drone flies.

The minimum cover required: €50 000 third-party liability. The annual premium from Latvian insurers (BTA, Balta, Gjensidige) typically runs €80–200 for recreational cover at this level.
</Scenario>

<CommonMistakes>
- Believing recreational use exempts the operator from insurance — it does not, unless the drone is C0 under 250 g.
- Confusing the mandatory third-party policy with hull cover — if the drone crashes and is damaged, the mandatory policy pays nothing.
- Letting the policy lapse between flying seasons without renewal — an expired policy means no legal flight.
- Using a policy bought for one country when operating in Latvia without verifying Latvian minimum cover levels — minimums vary by member state.
- Not carrying proof of insurance on-site — the policy must be available for inspection, not just exist somewhere at home.
</CommonMistakes>
