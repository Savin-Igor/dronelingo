# Critical-infrastructure sites

Latvia has sites whose security goes beyond ordinary privacy and
calls for special handling regardless of UAS class or subcategory.

## What "critical infrastructure" covers

- power stations and substations;
- water supply sites;
- major communications hubs;
- military sites;
- state-border technical facilities.

Each is marked with the sign:

> **"BEZ SASKAŅOŠANAS FOTOGRAFĒT, FILMĒT AIZLIEGTS"**
> ("Photography or filming forbidden without authorisation")

## Why this matters to the pilot

UGZ may not always cover these sites entirely — but **the filming
ban** works independently of UGZ status. Even when the drone flies
outside any prohibited zone, the camera frame **must not include**
a marked site without authorisation.

## Authorisation process

1. Identify the site operator (grid operator, military authority,
   etc.).
2. Submit an official **written** request with a flight plan and a
   copy of your insurance policy.
3. Get the **approval** before flying; do not start without it.

## Inadvertent exceptions

If a marked site enters the frame by accident, **delete the footage**
and inform the operator. Ignorance is not a defence — the sign and
the standard information bases (`airspace.lv`) provide sufficient
warning.

> **Source:** Latvian Critical Infrastructure Protection Law; CAA
> Latvia guidance; official sign wording.
