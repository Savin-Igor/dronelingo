## The basic rule: purpose first, retention second

If footage was collected for a defined purpose, it should be kept only for that purpose and only for as long as that purpose still exists.

For a drone operator, that means separating:

- **client deliverables**;
- **raw source footage**;
- **operational evidence** kept for safety, reporting, or insurance reasons;
- **material that should be deleted** because it has no continuing purpose.

Keeping everything forever is not a neutral choice. It increases privacy and security exposure.

## Raw footage is not automatically an archive

Operators often treat the SD card or export folder as a permanent archive. That is poor practice.

A better workflow is:

1. ingest footage into a structured project folder;
2. separate final deliverables from raw files;
3. keep only the minimum raw set needed for editing, contractual proof, or incident evidence;
4. delete duplicate or purposeless material on a defined schedule.

## Sharing creates new risk

Footage handling becomes more dangerous when operators:

- forward clips in open messaging apps,
- give the whole card dump to the client,
- sync raw footage automatically to uncontrolled cloud accounts,
- keep years of identifiable footage on unencrypted drives.

Every extra copy is another potential disclosure.

## When retention is justified

Longer retention may be justified where footage is needed for:

- a client contract,
- a dispute,
- an insurance claim,
- an incident report,
- a legal obligation.

But “maybe useful someday” is not a strong retention reason.

<Scenario title="After a construction shoot, the operator has 90 minutes of raw footage. The client needs only a 90-second edit. Several clips show neighbouring gardens and people who were not part of the job. What is the right handling approach?">
Keep the footage only according to purpose.

The operator should:

1. extract the material actually needed for the client deliverable;
2. identify whether some raw footage must be temporarily retained for editing or contractual proof;
3. delete irrelevant clips that contain unnecessary third-party exposure once they are no longer needed;
4. avoid giving the entire raw card dump to the client unless there is a defined contractual reason.

The goal is not zero retention. The goal is justified retention.
</Scenario>

- Sending full raw folders through consumer messaging apps.
- Treating all captured material as automatically belonging in the client package.

- Storing old footage on unencrypted drives with no retention schedule.
