# GDPR basics for drones

The moment a camera-equipped drone records an **identifiable person** —
even if the pilot did not intend the person as a subject — the General
Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies.

## Personal data from a drone

Personal data covers not only faces, but also:

- vehicle licence plates;
- voice recordings;
- gestures / behaviour that allow identifying a person in context.

## Lawful basis

GDPR requires a **lawful basis** (Art. 6) — typically:

- **consent** — the person agrees to be filmed;
- **legitimate interest** — e.g. inspection of a technical site, where
  any people in frame are incidental and not identifiable.

## Practical advice

- When flying near populated areas, **do not record video** unless
  necessary.
- If faces or plates enter the frame, **blur** them before publishing.
- Retain footage only as long as needed for the specific purpose.

## Latvia supervisor

In Latvia the supervisor is **Datu valsts inspekcija (DVI)**. Complaints
are investigated; fines can reach 4 % of annual turnover or €20 million
(whichever is higher).

> **Source:** Reg (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) Art. 4, 6; DVI guidance for
> drones.
