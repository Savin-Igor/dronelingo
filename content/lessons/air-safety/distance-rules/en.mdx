# Distance from uninvolved persons

## A1 — The closest you can get

A1 is designed for light drones that cause minimal harm if they fall.
But **no sub-category** removes the crowd rule.

| Class | Max weight | Distance to individuals | Assemblies |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|-----------|
| **C0** | < 250 g | Over individuals — **OK** | **Never** |
| **C1** | < 900 g | "Reasonably expected not to fly over" | **Never** |

"Reasonably expected" means: if there's a person ahead, you route around them.
If someone walks into your path, you abort.
C1 is not a licence to hover over a sunbather.

## A2 — When you need to get closer

A2 exists for professional work near people. It requires a separate competence certificate (theory + practical declaration) beyond A1/A3.

| Mode | Minimum horizontal distance |
|------|-----|
| C2 in **low-speed mode** (≤ 3 m/s) | **5 m** |
| C2 **normal speed** | **30 m** |

Low-speed mode is a mandatory hardware feature of C2 drones. You must activate it **before** closing to 5 m, not after.

## A3 — Far from civilisation

A3 is for larger drones (up to 25 kg) operating away from people, in areas that are genuinely uninhabited.

Three limits apply simultaneously — you must respect all three:

1. **1:1 rule** — horizontal distance to uninvolved persons ≥ flight altitude, minimum 30 m
2. **150 m** from any residential, commercial, industrial, or recreational area
3. **2-second flight distance** at max speed (crash-stop buffer)

If you are at 80 m altitude, the 1:1 rule requires ≥ 80 m to people.
The 30 m floor still applies at very low altitudes.

## The one rule that never changes

<MemoryAnchor
  rule="Overflight of assemblies of people is forbidden in every Open subcategory."
  hint="C0 under 250 g, C1, C2, C3 — doesn't matter. Crowd below = land or stay away."
/>

An "assembly of people" is not legally defined as a specific head count. Courts interpret it as any gathering where participants cannot easily move away from a drone overhead — a street market, a sports game, a concert, even a busy café terrace.

<Scenario title="A wedding photographer asks you to fly a C1 (780 g) over the ceremony for an overhead shot. Is this permitted?">
**No.** A wedding ceremony is an assembly of people. C1 is still subject to the "no overflight of assemblies" rule in A1. The weight of the drone does not override this limit.

The only legal overhead shot of a crowd in the Open category would require an operating permit in the Specific category — which is a different regulatory path entirely.
</Scenario>

<CommonMistakes>
- Thinking C0 (< 250 g) allows flights over crowds — the assembly rule applies to all Open sub-categories.
- Confusing the 1:1 rule with a 1 m per kg formula — it has nothing to do with drone weight.
- Activating low-speed mode mid-approach to 5 m instead of before.
- Applying A1 rules while operating a C2 drone — your drone class determines which sub-category applies, not what you prefer.
- Thinking 150 m from residential areas means 150 m from every individual building — it means the **area**, not the fence line of a single house.
</CommonMistakes>
