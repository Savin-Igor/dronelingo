# Frequently asked questions

## Is dronelingo an official CAA Latvia course?

No. dronelingo is an **independent prep tool** for the A1/A3 exam.
The official course and exam live at
[e.caa.gov.lv](https://e.caa.gov.lv/). We help you understand the
material and rehearse so you pass the real exam first try.

## How much does it cost?

**Nothing unless you pass.** Learn and practise for free. The €19 is
only charged after you pass the official A1/A3 exam and opt into our
Pass Guarantee. Don't pass — don't pay.

## Is the content up to date?

Content is grounded in Reg (EU) 2019/947 (consolidated 2022-04-04),
Reg (EU) 2019/945 and EASA Easy Access Rules for UAS (2024-07).
Latvia-specific details are based on the public CAA Latvia drone site,
[droni.caa.gov.lv](https://droni.caa.gov.lv/), and were last reviewed
on 2026-05-08. Every lesson and exam question cites the official
source it is based on.

## Why three languages (lv / en / ru)?

Latvia's drone community is multilingual. The official exam **is in
Latvian only**, but prep materials in other languages let you build
intuition before meeting the official wording.

## Do I need an account?

Not yet. Your practice history stays in your own browser on this
device. After you pass the real exam and choose Pass Guarantee we'll
only ask for an email so we can send a thank-you note.

## What happens after I pass?

CAA Latvia automatically issues an **A1/A3 knowledge certificate
PDF**, valid for 5 years and recognised across the EU. You may then
fly in Open-category A1 and A3 sub-categories under the standard
rules.

## Do I have to register if my drone weighs less than 250 g?

If it has a photo/video camera — **yes**, register as a UAS operator.
Without a camera — no. See the [registration guide](/guide).

## Does the certificate work in other EU countries?

Yes, the A1/A3 certificate is **recognised across the EU**. When
flying in another EU country you must still respect that country's
geographical zones and local rules.

## My drone is old and has no C-class marking — what now?

Transitional rules place legacy drones by mass: < 250 g flies as C0
(A1), < 25 kg as C3 (A3).

## What about occurrence reporting?

Serious consequences (injury, near-miss with a manned aircraft) —
**report within 72 hours** at
[e.caa.gov.lv/incidents](https://e.caa.gov.lv/incidents). See our
**Security** topic for the full picture.

## Is content available offline?

Not yet. Offline access is planned for a later update.
