Most first-time drone buyers walk into the question "which one should I buy?" thinking about camera specs and flight time. The bigger question is **weight class**, because weight decides where you can legally fly, which licence you need, and what insurance is required.

This is a pre-purchase walk-through of the four weight classes that matter for the first buyer.

## The four classes that matter

Under EU rules (Reg 2019/945), drones sold after January 2024 carry one of these labels printed on the airframe:

| Class | Max mass | Where you can fly |
|---|---|---|
| **C0** | < 250 g | A1 — over people but never crowds |
| **C1** | < 900 g | A1 — close to people, no overflight |
| **C2** | < 4 kg | A2 — within 30 m of people |
| **C3** | < 25 kg | A3 — far from populated areas |

C4 also exists (FPV/competition variant of C3) but is irrelevant for the first-time photographer or videographer.

## C0 (< 250 g) — the airport-bypass class

C0 is the sweet spot for travel and casual photography. Under 250 g, the drone:

- Can be flown **over uninvolved people** (never crowds) without prior notice
- Requires no operator registration **if** it has no camera
- With a camera, still requires operator registration (because of GDPR), but **no theory exam** in some interpretations — though Latvia treats sub-250 g with camera as A1, which needs A1/A3 anyway

Typical examples:

- **DJI Mini 4 Pro** (~249 g) — premium sub-250 g, 4K HDR
- **DJI Mini SE** (~249 g) — budget sub-250 g
- **Autel EVO Nano+** (~249 g)

For a first drone in 2026, this is the default recommendation **unless you specifically need longer range or larger sensors**.

## C1 (< 900 g) — the price/performance sweet spot

C1 drones are heavier but still in A1, with restrictions: you can fly close to people but not directly over them. C1 drones typically have:

- Larger sensor (1-inch or close)
- Better wind resistance
- Longer transmission range (10+ km)
- 30–40 min flight time

Typical examples:

- **DJI Air 3** (~720 g)
- **DJI Mavic 3 Classic** (~895 g)
- **Autel EVO Lite+** (~835 g)

C1 is the right pick if you need image quality beyond what a sub-250 g sensor can produce, and you fly mostly in unpopulated outdoor settings.

## C2 (< 4 kg) — only if you commercialise

C2 drones are explicitly designed for **A2 operations** — close to people. They include a hardware low-speed switch (3 m/s mode) that earns the ≤ 5 m distance allowance under A2.

C2 drones are typically used by:

- Real-estate photographers
- Wedding videographers
- Roof / facade inspectors
- Industrial surveyors

Typical examples:

- **DJI Mavic 3 Pro** (~960 g, sometimes ≥1 kg in Cine version)
- **DJI Inspire 3** (~3 kg, professional cinema)

C2 requires **A2 certification** ([guide](/blog/eu-open-category-a1-a2-a3-explained)) in addition to A1/A3, and is overkill for a hobbyist.

## C3 (< 25 kg) — heavy lifting only

C3 covers everything from a heavy 6 kg photography rig to a 24 kg agricultural sprayer. Only operates in A3 (far from populated areas) under Open. In Latvia, the realistic use cases are:

- Land surveying / mapping
- Agricultural inspection
- Search and rescue (volunteer)
- Heavy cinema rigs (rare)

Most first-time buyers should not consider C3. If you do, you almost certainly need to operate in **Specific category** anyway.

## What about pre-2024 drones (no C-class)?

Drones manufactured before January 2024 without a C-class label fall into **legacy** rules:

- **Sub-250 g without Remote ID**: same as C0
- **250 g – 900 g without Remote ID**: restricted to **A3 only** from 2024
- **Above 900 g without Remote ID**: restricted to A3 only

If you are buying second-hand, **a 2022 DJI Mavic Air 2 without Remote ID is now A3-only**. You will not be able to fly it in a city. Check for a C-class sticker or post-2024 manufacture date before buying.

## Decision tree

1. Want to travel with the drone, fly anywhere, casual photography? **→ C0**
2. Want better image quality, mostly outdoors? **→ C1**
3. Want to commercialise close to people / urban work? **→ C2 + A2 exam**
4. Heavy industrial / agricultural? **→ C3 + likely Specific category**

For 95% of first-time buyers reading this, the answer is **C0 or C1**.

## Common mistakes

1. **Buying a heavy drone for "future-proofing".** Each weight class up adds restrictions, exams, and cost. Buy the lightest drone that fits your actual use.

2. **Buying second-hand without checking C-class.** Pre-2024 drones without Remote ID are A3-only. Verify before paying.

3. **Assuming "small drone = no rules".** Sub-250 g with a camera still requires operator registration and (in Latvia's interpretation) an A1/A3 certificate.
