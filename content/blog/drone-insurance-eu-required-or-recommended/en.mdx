In Latvia, drone insurance is not a simple yes-or-no question based only on whether the flight is “commercial”. The binding answer comes from the current Latvian insurance rules and the CAA matrix published for drone operations.

## What the mandatory insurance is about

The CAA guidance is about **third-party civil liability**. In plain terms, that means damage caused to:

- other people
- other people’s property
- the environment, where applicable

It is **not** the same thing as insurance for your own drone. Damage to your aircraft, payload, or business losses is a separate question.

## The Latvian minimums are not one flat number

The public CAA insurance page does **not** reduce everything to one universal threshold. It publishes a matrix by category, class, and mass.

A simplified reading of the current CAA table is:

| Operation | Current minimum on the CAA table |
|---|---|
| Open category A1 with `<250 g` or `C0` | no minimum listed |
| Open category with `C1` / `C2`, and A3 operations below `20 kg` | `50,000 EUR` |
| Open category A3 with `20–25 kg` aircraft | `750,000 SDR` |
| Specific category from `250 g` to `500 kg` | `750,000 SDR` |

If your operation sits near a boundary, the right move is to check the official CAA page directly instead of relying on generic EU summaries.

## Why this matters

Two common mistakes create problems:

1. assuming that “hobby” automatically means “no insurance requirement”
2. assuming that one EU-wide number applies to every drone case in Latvia

The Latvian CAA material is more specific than that. The minimum depends on the operation and aircraft characteristics.

## What to check before buying a policy

Before you treat a policy as sufficient, confirm:

- that it is **third-party liability** cover
- the **territorial scope**
- the **liability limit**
- whether the policy matches the way you actually fly

That last point matters especially if you operate across different countries or move from simple Open-category flights to more demanding work.

## Cross-border flights

The CAA also reminds operators to check the destination state before flying abroad. For lighter drones, insurance requirements are not harmonised in exactly the same way across all European states.

So the safe rule is:

- check Latvia for flights in Latvia
- check the destination country before cross-border flights

## A practical way to treat insurance

If your operation falls inside a mandatory-insurance case, keep the policy details organised before flight:

- insurer name
- policy number
- validity dates
- liability limit

That saves time when registering, updating documentation, or explaining your setup after an incident.
