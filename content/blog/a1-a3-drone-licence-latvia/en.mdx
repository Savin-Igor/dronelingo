In Latvia, A1/A3 is not a generic “camera-drone licence”. It is the qualification the CAA requires for certain flights in the Open category.

## When A1/A3 is actually required

The Latvian CAA page for the online course says A1/A3 training and the exam are required before flight if the drone:

- has a **C1, C2, C3 or C4** class mark, or
- has a mass of **250 g or more**

That is the main legal threshold.

If you fly a drone under 250 g, the position is narrower:

- a camera may still trigger **operator registration**
- but A1/A3 does **not** become mandatory just because a camera is present

So before treating A1/A3 as automatic, separate three questions:

1. Do I need **operator registration**?
2. Do I need **pilot qualification**?
3. Which **subcategory** am I flying in?

## Before you start

The CAA lists one practical prerequisite for the Latvian A1/A3 course:

- a Latvian **remote-pilot number** in the portal

So the normal order is:

1. register in `e.caa.gov.lv`
2. obtain the required operator and pilot registrations
3. then start the A1/A3 course

## What the course includes

The official online course is **free** and has **no time limit**. The published syllabus covers nine topics:

1. air safety
2. airspace limitations
3. aviation regulation
4. human performance limitations
5. operational procedures
6. general UAS knowledge
7. privacy and data protection
8. insurance
9. security

## Exam format

The public CAA page states:

- **40 minutes**
- **40 questions**
- at least **75% correct** to pass

After a successful pass, the certificate becomes available in the CAA UAS information system. The certificate is valid for **5 years** and recognised across the **European Union**.

## What A1/A3 lets you do

A1/A3 covers the two Open-category subcategories named in the certificate:

- **A1** — lighter aircraft and stricter rules around uninvolved people
- **A3** — flights far from uninvolved people and at least `150 m` from residential, commercial, industrial, or recreational areas

If your planned operation is closer to people with a `C2` drone, that is **A2**, not A1/A3 alone.

## A2 is separate

The Latvian CAA treats A2 as a separate step:

- valid **A1/A3** proof first
- practical self-training
- separate **A2** exam

That means passing A1/A3 does **not** automatically authorise A2 flights.

## How to prepare without wasting time

Most candidates do not lose time on obscure aviation theory. They lose it on Open-category basics they read too quickly.

Focus on:

- the difference between **operator** and **remote pilot**
- when **registration** is required
- **VLOS** and FPV limits
- **120 m** height limit
- **A1, A2, A3** distance rules
- Latvian **geographical zones**

If you can explain those rules in your own words, you are usually preparing in the right way.
