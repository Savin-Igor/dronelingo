"Can I fly here?" is the question every Latvian drone pilot asks before take-off — and the right answer depends on where *here* is. Latvia has four overlapping categories of restricted airspace that can shut down your flight: **airport CTRs**, **military training areas**, **national parks**, and **temporary NOTAM restrictions**. Get any of them wrong and you risk fines starting around €70 — or, in airport CTRs, criminal charges.

## The four categories

| Type | Boundary | What changes |
|---|---|---|
| **Airport CTR** | Circular zone around an airport (typical 9.3 km radius) | Drone flight requires ATC authorisation |
| **Military training area** | Hex-shaped zones marked on AIP charts | Closed when active, see NOTAM |
| **National parks** | Polygon-shaped protected territories | Restricted; permit required from park administration |
| **NOTAM** | Temporary, anywhere | Closes airspace for events, presidential movements, search and rescue |

## Airport CTRs — the hardest stop

Latvia has four civil airports with active Control Zones (CTRs):

- **Riga (EVRA)** — 9.3 km radius, covers Mārupe, Babīte, parts of Pārdaugava
- **Liepāja (EVLA)** — 6 km radius
- **Daugavpils (EVDA)** — 5 km radius (military / civil shared)
- **Ventspils (EVVA)** — 5 km radius

Inside a CTR, drone flight is **prohibited** unless you obtain explicit authorisation from the airport's Air Traffic Control. For occasional hobby use this is impractical — even a request takes 1–2 weeks. The realistic plan is to fly outside the CTR.

CTRs extend to ground level for drones (unlike general aviation, where a CTR may start at 1500 ft). There is no "fly under" exception.

## Military training areas

The Latvian Armed Forces operate several training areas across the country. The largest is **Ādaži** (north-east of Riga, ~24 km × 14 km), with smaller ones at **Lāčusils**, **Mežainie tīreļi**, and the coastal **Skrunda** range. Each area is published as a hex-zone on the AIP chart with activation times (typically weekdays 08:00–17:00, sometimes longer for exercises).

When active, military areas are **closed to all civil aviation**, drones included. Outside activation hours they revert to normal airspace, but you must verify activation status via the daily NOTAM before flying near them. The **Lielvārde Air Base** circles add a permanent restriction zone.

## National parks and protected areas

Latvia's national parks have their own drone rules, set by the park administration rather than CAA:

- **Gauja National Park** — drone flight restricted, permit required
- **Slītere National Park** — drone flight restricted
- **Ķemeri National Park** — drone flight restricted near wetlands during bird breeding
- **Rāzna National Park** — restricted in core zones

These restrictions are **not** about safety — they are about protecting wildlife. Penalties are administrative fines under environmental protection law. Permits are case-by-case and require justification (research, journalism, etc.); pure hobby photography is rarely approved.

## NOTAM — the floating restriction

A **NOTAM** (Notice to Airmen) is a temporary airspace closure issued for events, security details, search-and-rescue operations, or any reason airspace needs to be cleared. NOTAMs override everything else: even an open A3 area can become a no-fly zone for a few hours.

NOTAMs in Latvia are published at:

- **eans.lgs.lv** — Latvian AIS, daily NOTAM bulletin
- Most drone apps (DJI Fly, Litchi, etc.) display active NOTAMs as a UI layer

Always check before take-off. NOTAMs are the most common reason a flight that "should be legal" suddenly is not.

## Riga specifically

Riga is the hardest city to fly in. Combined restrictions:

- **EVRA CTR**: 9.3 km from runway, covering Mārupe, Babīte, Pārdaugava, parts of Imanta
- **Helicopter routes** at Stradiņš Hospital and the river
- **Old Town no-fly**: separate municipal restriction over the UNESCO-protected historic center
- **Presidential / parliamentary** ad-hoc NOTAMs near Saeima and the Riga Castle

In practice, almost no part of central Riga is drone-legal under the Open category. Hobbyists fly outside the CTR — Carnikava, Babīte (north of CTR), or further into the Pierīga region.

## How to check before take-off

Three sources, in order of trust:

1. **Latvian AIS daily NOTAM** at eans.lgs.lv — authoritative
2. **droni.caa.gov.lv UTM map** — official drone-specific overlay
3. **Drone manufacturer apps** — DJI Fly, Autel Explorer — convenient but not authoritative; treat as a sanity check

For commercial work, all three. For hobby, at minimum (1) on the morning of the flight.

## Common mistakes

1. **Trusting the drone app alone.** Manufacturer apps update zones with delays of weeks. Always cross-check with the AIS NOTAM.

2. **Assuming national park boundary == sign at the entrance.** Park boundaries on the map extend further than the visible signage. Check the actual polygon.

3. **Ignoring military NOTAMs because the area "looks empty."** Empty fields near Ādaži are training areas. The fact that there is no fence does not mean there is no airspace restriction.
