The EU Open category is where nearly every hobby and light commercial drone flight in Europe sits. Inside Open, the regulation splits operations into three subcategories — **A1, A2, and A3** — each with its own rules on what you can fly, where, and how close to people. The differences look small at a glance but cost you the most points on the [A1/A3 exam](/exam) if you get them wrong.

## The 30-second summary

| | A1 | A2 | A3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Where | Anywhere, including built-up areas | Built-up areas at ≥ 30 m from uninvolved people | Far from people: ≥ 150 m from residential, commercial, industrial, or recreational areas |
| Drone classes | C0, C1 | C2 | C2 (slow mode), C3, C4 |
| Max take-off mass | < 900 g | < 4 kg | < 25 kg |
| Theory exam | A1/A3 (40 Q) | A1/A3 + A2 supplement | A1/A3 (40 Q) |
| Practical | — | Self-declared | — |

## A1 — fly close, but never over crowds

A1 is the most permissive subcategory **regarding distance to people**, but it limits drone size. You can fly C0 drones (under 250 g, sub-19 m/s) directly over uninvolved bystanders. With a C1 drone (under 900 g), you may fly close to people but **must not overfly them** intentionally. Crowds (assemblies of people) are never allowed under A1 regardless of drone class.

This is where most hobby pilots end up: a DJI Mini-class drone for park photography in a residential area.

## A2 — closer to people, but only with extra training

A2 is the middle tier and the only one requiring an **additional exam** beyond A1/A3. You may fly a C2 drone (under 4 kg) as close as **30 m horizontally** to uninvolved people — or 5 m if the drone has a low-speed switch engaged. A2 is the natural step for commercial work in urban environments: real-estate photography, inspections, small events.

To earn A2 you must:

1. Hold the A1/A3 certificate (a prerequisite)
2. Pass the A2 supplementary theory exam (a separate 30-question test)
3. Submit a self-declaration of practical training (no instructor required — you certify your own competency)

The A2 certificate, like A1/A3, is valid five years.

## A3 — heavy drones, but only far from people

A3 lets you fly the heaviest legal drones in Open — up to 25 kg — but only in **unpopulated areas**. The exact wording: at least 150 m horizontally from any residential, commercial, industrial, or recreational area. In practice this means fields, forests, or unzoned countryside, not parks.

A3 also enforces the **1:1 rule**: horizontal distance from any uninvolved person must be at least equal to the drone's altitude, with a hard floor of 30 m. Flying at 80 m AGL means staying at least 80 m horizontally from any bystander.

Crowds are forbidden in every subcategory — there is no "A4 over crowds." Filming a concert or a festival under Open is not legal, regardless of drone size or licence.

## Which subcategory needs which licence?

This trips up many candidates: the **same A1/A3 certificate covers both A1 and A3**. There is no separate "A1 only" or "A3 only" licence. The single 40-question exam at [e.caa.gov.lv](/blog/a1-a3-drone-licence-latvia) qualifies you for both.

A2 is the only subcategory that needs its own additional exam. If you only fly C0 (sub-250 g) drones, even A1/A3 may technically be optional — but the moment a camera is involved, you need the operator registration and the A1/A3 certificate anyway.

## The C-class system

Open relies on **drone C-classes** to set boundaries. A C-class is a label given to the drone at manufacture (after 2024) based on weight, max speed, kinetic energy, and required safety features:

- **C0**: under 250 g, max 19 m/s, no certificate needed for sub-250 g without camera
- **C1**: under 900 g, max 19 m/s, fly under A1 with A1/A3 licence
- **C2**: under 4 kg, low-speed switch required, fly under A2 with A2 licence
- **C3 / C4**: under 25 kg, fly under A3 with A1/A3 licence

Drones manufactured before January 2024 without a C-class label fall into the **legacy** rules: a different table that mostly maps cleanly onto the C-classes but is worth checking case by case.

## Common mistakes

1. **Assuming A2 lets you fly over crowds.** It does not. A2 reduces minimum horizontal distance from people, but the crowd ban applies everywhere in Open.

2. **Thinking the A1/A3 exam only qualifies you for one or the other.** One exam, both subcategories.

3. **Flying a heavy drone in a built-up area on the assumption that A1/A3 alone is enough.** Heavy drones (C2 and above) in built-up environments require A2. Without A2 you can still fly a C2 drone in A3 (far from people) but not in A1 or A2 environments.
