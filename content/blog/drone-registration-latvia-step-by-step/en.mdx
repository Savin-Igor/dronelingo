Before you fly any drone heavier than 250 g — or any drone with a camera, no matter how light — you must register as a **UAS operator** with Latvia's Civil Aviation Agency (CAA). The good news: it is online, free, and done in about ten minutes. The catch: most people register *the drone* when they should be registering *themselves*.

## What you actually register

Under EU Regulation 2019/947, registration is done by the **operator** — the person legally responsible for the drone — not by the drone itself. One operator number covers every drone you own. If you buy a second drone next year, you reuse the same number.

A separate registration as a **remote pilot** exists implicitly through your A1/A3 certificate ([details here](/blog/a1-a3-drone-licence-latvia)). For most hobby pilots, operator and pilot are the same person.

## When registration is required

You must register if:

- Your drone has a **sensor capable of capturing personal data** (any camera) — this includes sub-250 g drones like the DJI Mini series
- Your drone weighs **250 g or more**, regardless of camera
- You are operating under the Specific category (not relevant for hobby use)

Sub-250 g drones without a camera flown over private property without other people present are technically exempt. In practice, almost no consumer drone meets that bar.

## What you need before you start

- **Latvian electronic identification**: eID card, eParaksts mobile, or Smart-ID
- A working email address
- For commercial use only: civil-liability insurance details (see our [insurance article](/blog/drone-insurance-eu-required-or-recommended))

You do **not** need the drone in front of you. The drone's serial number is not required at this step — it is for the operator, not the device.

## Step 1 — log in to e.caa.gov.lv

Open [https://e.caa.gov.lv](https://e.caa.gov.lv) and click "Authenticate". You will be redirected to **Latvija.lv**, the national identity broker. Choose your preferred method (eID, eParaksts mobile, or Smart-ID), authenticate, and you will return to the portal as a verified user.

If you do not have eID or Smart-ID set up, this step will block you. There is no paper alternative.

## Step 2 — open the UAS section and start operator registration

In the portal navigation, look for **Unmanned Aircraft / UAS / Droni**. The English UI labels these slightly differently than the Latvian — both routes lead to the same forms.

Inside the UAS section, click **"New operator registration"**. The form asks for:

- Your full legal name (pre-filled from your eID)
- Mailing address
- Phone number
- Email
- Confirmation of age (≥ 16, or younger with a registered legal guardian)
- Insurance details (commercial only)

Submit the form. Processing is essentially instant; you receive the operator number on the next screen and in a confirmation email.

## Step 3 — your operator number

The number is in the format **`LVA` + 12 hex/alphanumeric characters**, for example `LVA3a4b5c6d7e8f`. Save it. You will:

- Mark every drone you own with this number
- Enter it into the **Remote ID** field on any drone that supports Remote ID (most C-class drones do)
- Reference it on any A1/A3 or A2 exam attempt and on insurance documents

The number is **personal and reusable**. Do not let other operators use it; legally they would be operating as you.

## Step 4 — mark the drone

Latvian CAA does not specify exact dimensions, but the marking must be:

- Visible **without removing any cover or panel**
- **Indelible** — not a pencil or marker that washes off; a printed sticker or laser engraving is fine
- On a **fireproof part** of the drone if practical, so the marking survives a crash

A 20 × 40 mm laminated sticker on the underside of the main body, near the battery compartment but not on the battery itself, is the typical convention.

## Step 5 — register Remote ID (if applicable)

If your drone has a **C1, C2, C3, or C5 class label**, it includes a Remote ID transmitter. You must enter your operator number into the drone's onboard settings using the manufacturer's app. The Remote ID broadcasts the operator number live during flight, allowing law-enforcement and other airspace users to identify the operator.

Sub-250 g drones (C0) without Remote ID are exempt. Older legacy drones without Remote ID and without a C-class are restricted to A3-only flight from 2024 onwards.

## After registration

Your registration is valid **for one year** and renews automatically when you log into the portal annually. You do not need a new operator number — only confirmation that your contact details are current.
