## Fees in 2026

The vast majority of A1/A3 hobbyist services are **free**. Fees mostly apply to commercial Specific-category operations.

### Free of charge

- Operator registration (and renewal)
- A1/A3 theory exam attempts
- A1/A3 certificate
- A2 theory exam attempts and certificate
- Issuance of an operator number
- Standard NOTAM bulletins

### Paid services

| Service | Fee |
|---|---|
| Specific-category Operational Authorisation (standard scenario) | from €430 |
| Operational Authorisation amendment | from €110 |
| Light UAS Operator Certificate (LUC) issuance | from €1 200 |
| Special exemption / waiver application | from €350 |
| Duplicate / replacement of certificate | €15 |
| Geographical-zone authorisation (one-off flight in a restricted area) | from €70 |

These are state-fee tariffs and can change annually — confirm on caa.gov.lv before submitting.

## Process timelines

These are typical times. Add buffer if you are submitting during summer holidays (July–August) or year-end (mid-December to mid-January).

| Process | Typical time |
|---|---|
| Operator registration | Instant |
| A1/A3 / A2 certificate generation | Within minutes of passing |
| Specific Operational Authorisation (standard scenario) | 30 working days |
| Specific Operational Authorisation (PDRA) | 15 working days |
| Specific custom-scenario (SORA) | 90+ working days |
| Geographical-zone permit | 14 working days |
| Operator number replacement | 5 working days |

If you are flying commercially in cells like real-estate photography, the realistic onboarding clock to be "fully legal" is **30–45 days** end-to-end (registration, A2 + A1/A3, insurance procurement, geographical-zone permits if needed).

## Where to find official information

In order of authority:

1. **caa.gov.lv** — official CAA website. Latvian and English.
2. **droni.caa.gov.lv** — drone-specific portal with rule summaries, news, and the UTM map.
3. **eans.lgs.lv** — Latvian AIS (Air Information Service) for NOTAMs and AIP charts.
4. **e.caa.gov.lv** — operator self-service: registration, exams, certificates.

Manufacturer apps and general Google searches are **not** authoritative. Always cross-check against caa.gov.lv.

## Common questions

**Q: Can I register as a foreign citizen living in Latvia?**
A: Yes, but you need a Latvian eID or eParaksts mobile. Without electronic ID, no registration is possible; CAA does not accept paper for new registrations.

**Q: Does my A1/A3 certificate work in Estonia or Lithuania?**
A: Yes. EASA certificates are recognised EU-wide under Reg 2019/947. The operator registration is national — re-register if you change your country of residence.

**Q: What happens if I fail the A1/A3 exam ten times?**
A: Nothing administrative — no waiting period, no fee, no penalty. The system just keeps logging attempts. The practical advice: stop and study after two consecutive fails.

**Q: How do I appeal a fine?**
A: Submit a written appeal to CAA's legal department within 30 days of the fine notice. Email: legal@caa.gov.lv. Include the case number and your justification. Decisions take 60–90 days.

**Q: Where do I report a near-miss with a manned aircraft?**
A: Mandatory voluntary reporting via [eccairs.aero](https://eccairs.aero) or CAA's incident form. Confidential and recommended even if no damage occurred — it feeds Latvian airspace safety statistics.
