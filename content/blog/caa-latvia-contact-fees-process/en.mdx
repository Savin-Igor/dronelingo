If you need a current contact, a confirmed fee, or the right reporting channel, use the official CAA sources first. This page is a practical map to those sources, not a substitute for them.

## Main CAA contact details

The official CAA contact page lists:

- **Address:** `Biroju iela 10, Lidosta Rīga, Mārupes novads, LV-1053`
- **General phone:** `+371 67830936`
- **General email:** `caa@caa.gov.lv`
- **Working hours:** Monday to Friday `08:00–12:00` and `12:30–16:30`

For drone-specific questions, the dedicated contact is:

- **Email:** `droni@caa.gov.lv`
- **Drone portal:** `droni.caa.gov.lv`
- **Self-service portal:** `e.caa.gov.lv`

On the drone contact page, CAA also publishes topic-based phone numbers for registration, qualifications, geographical zones, and Specific-category operations. If your question is not urgent, email is usually the cleanest route because it leaves a written trail.

## Fees that are clearly published

The public CAA pages currently state:

- **UAS operator registration:** `5 EUR`
- **Remote-pilot portal registration:** free
- **A1/A3 online course and exam:** free
- **A2 exam at CAA:** `15 EUR` for the first attempt, `10 EUR` for a repeat attempt

The A2 page also notes that recognised organisations may set **their own prices**. So if you take the exam outside the CAA itself, check the provider before booking.

## What not to treat as a fixed rule

For other paid services — especially in the Specific category — do not rely on old blog tables, forum screenshots, or copied price lists. Use:

1. the official **CAA tariff page**
2. the relevant **service page**
3. the current list of **recognised organisations**, if the service is delegated

That matters because fees, lists of providers, and application routes do change.

## Exams and qualification

If your goal is Open-category flying, the practical split is simple:

- start with [operator registration](/blog/drone-registration-latvia-step-by-step)
- if your drone or operation requires it, complete [A1/A3](/blog/a1-a3-drone-licence-latvia)
- if you need A2, check whether you want to sit it with the CAA directly or through a recognised organisation

The official qualification pages are better than second-hand summaries, because they show the current prerequisites, language, and certificate validity.

## Incident reporting

For safety occurrences, the CAA materials point pilots to:

- `https://e.caa.gov.lv/incidents`
- `https://e2.aviationreporting.eu/reporting`

The purpose of occurrence reporting is safety improvement, not blame assignment. The public CAA guidance also distinguishes between mandatory reporting and voluntary reporting.

## Best verification path

When you need the current answer, check in this order:

1. `caa.gov.lv` for official contacts and tariff information
2. `droni.caa.gov.lv` for drone-specific rules, qualifications, insurance, and geographical zones
3. `e.caa.gov.lv` for actual registration, qualifications, certificates, and incident forms
