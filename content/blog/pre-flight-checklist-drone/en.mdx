A pre-flight checklist is the difference between a routine flight and an expensive mistake. EASA does not mandate a specific form, but every certified pilot is expected to verify a basic set of conditions before take-off. Here is the simplest 12-item list that catches 95% of avoidable problems.

## Print this, take it with you

A laminated card in your drone case beats a mental checklist every time. Print, laminate, tick before flight.

## Weather (4 items)

**1. Wind speed and gusts.** Check both **mean wind** and **gust factor**. Your drone's manufacturer specifies a maximum (often 10–15 m/s for hobby drones). Gusts can be double the mean and overpower the drone in seconds. Source: a weather app showing surface wind plus a glance at the Beaufort scale outside.

**2. Visibility.** EASA Open requires VLOS. In practice this means **horizontal visibility ≥ 5 km** to spot other aircraft. Fog, heavy rain, snow — flight not allowed. See our [no-fly conditions](/blog/no-fly-zones-latvia) for related airspace restrictions.

**3. Precipitation.** Most consumer drones are **not waterproof**. Even light rain in the wrong wind direction shorts the motor windings. Forecast must be dry from take-off to estimated landing time + 30 min buffer.

**4. Temperature.** Lithium batteries lose ~30% capacity below 5 °C and risk thermal shutdown below 0 °C. Above 35 °C, motors derate. Both extremes shorten flight time and may trigger force-landing.

## Location (3 items)

**5. Airspace clearance.** Verify against AIS NOTAM and the [Latvia restricted-zone map](/blog/no-fly-zones-latvia). CTRs (Riga, Liepāja, Daugavpils, Ventspils), military areas, national parks all override your flight plan.

**6. People and property.** Walk the area. Identify uninvolved people, parked cars, livestock. In A3, maintain ≥ 150 m from residential / commercial / recreational areas and the 1:1 rule (horizontal distance ≥ altitude, ≥ 30 m). In A1, no overflight of uninvolved people with C1+. In A2, ≥ 30 m horizontal from people.

**7. Take-off and emergency landing zones.** Identify (a) where you take off and (b) where the drone will go if it loses signal or RTH activates. The "fail-safe RTH altitude" must be above local obstacles.

## Drone (3 items)

**8. Battery state.** Both drone and controller batteries ≥ 80% to give margin. Check for **swelling** — a swollen LiPo is one flight away from a fire. Spare battery in case the flight extends.

**9. Propellers.** Visual check each blade for nicks, cracks, and tight rotation. Replace as a set, not individually — mismatched propeller wear causes vibration that ages the motors.

**10. Drone firmware and Remote ID.** Open the manufacturer's app. Confirm firmware current, no warnings, operator number ([how to get one](/blog/drone-registration-latvia-step-by-step)) entered correctly in Remote ID settings.

## Paperwork (2 items)

**11. Certificate validity.** Your A1/A3 certificate ([guide](/blog/a1-a3-drone-licence-latvia)) is good for 5 years. Check expiry date.

**12. Insurance validity.** Active policy with declared liability limit, valid for the flight location ([insurance guide](/blog/drone-insurance-eu-required-or-recommended)). Commercial flights: confirm policy covers the specific operation type.

## The 30-second version

If you only have 30 seconds (you shouldn't, but if):

1. Wind under maximum, no gusts
2. Visibility ≥ 5 km, no rain
3. NOTAM clear
4. People accounted for, ≥ required distance
5. Battery ≥ 80%, propellers visual-OK
6. Operator number in Remote ID
7. Certificates valid

## Common mistakes

1. **Skipping NOTAM "because it's the same field as last week".** NOTAMs change daily.

2. **Ignoring gusts because the mean is calm.** A 5 m/s mean with 12 m/s gusts is unsafe for sub-900 g drones.

3. **Trusting the drone's "Ready to Fly" indicator alone.** It checks the drone, not the airspace, weather, or law.
