The A1/A3 theory exam at e.caa.gov.lv is the only legal step between operator registration and flying a camera-equipped drone in Latvia. It is **40 questions, 40 minutes, ≥75% to pass** — and it is harder than people expect. Here is what to expect on test day and how to prepare for it.

## The format in one breath

- **40 multiple-choice questions** — exactly one correct answer each, no partial credit
- **40 minutes total** — you can navigate freely between questions, change answers until you submit
- **≥75% pass mark** — that means at least 30 of 40 correct
- **Unlimited retakes**, each one free
- **Online**, taken from your own device, no proctor

## Question distribution

The 40 questions are stratified across the nine EASA topic areas. Approximate weighting based on the CAA Latvia question bank:

| Topic | Questions (approx.) |
|---|---|
| Air safety | 7 |

This is **not** uniform — Air Safety alone is nearly 20% of the exam. Spend study time accordingly.

## What the questions look like

A typical question:

> A pilot is flying a 700 g C1 drone in A1 over a park. A jogger runs into the flight area. What is the correct action?
>
> a) Continue — A1 allows flight over uninvolved people.
> b) Land immediately and wait for the jogger to clear the area.
> c) Increase altitude to 120 m to maintain VLOS.
> d) Switch to A2 mode in the controller.

(Correct: b. A1 with C1 does not allow intentional overflight of uninvolved people, even though the subcategory permits proximity.)

Questions are factual, scenario-based, or numerical. About 20% involve simple arithmetic — battery percentages, minimum distances, threshold calculations.

## What CAA grades hard

Three patterns produce the most wrong answers:

1. **VLOS edge cases.** FPV without observer, "I can see roughly where it is," using a phone screen for navigation. All wrong.

2. **Numerical thresholds.** 30 m horizontal distance in A2. 150 m from populated areas in A3. 120 m AGL maximum. Memorize the numbers.

3. **Operator vs pilot vs Remote ID.** Three separate concepts. The exam tests the distinction explicitly.

## Day-of-exam workflow

**Step 1.** Log into e.caa.gov.lv via your eID / eParaksts / Smart-ID.

**Step 2.** Navigate to UAS → Exams → "Start A1/A3 theory exam".

**Step 3.** Read the brief. The system shows confidentiality and good-faith terms. Accept.

**Step 4.** Timer starts at 40 minutes. Questions appear one at a time. Use the **jump grid** to navigate; flag uncertain answers and return.

**Step 5.** Submit. Result is instant — pass/fail, score, per-topic breakdown.

**Step 6.** On pass, the certificate is generated within seconds and stored in your profile as a downloadable PDF.

On fail, you may immediately retake, but the system recommends a 24-hour pause for reflection (which is good advice — repeat failures within an hour have low success rates).

## Common mistakes

1. **Sitting cold.** The exam is harder than the regulation text suggests. Walking in without practice is a 60% pass rate at best, even for engineers.

2. **Memorising sample questions instead of concepts.** CAA Latvia rotates the bank. Sample questions help you recognise patterns; they do not predict the actual exam.

3. **Rushing the answer-review phase.** With 40 minutes for 40 questions, you have one minute per question. Use the spare minutes at the end to review flagged answers.
