# dronelingo.eu

An online preparation platform for the EASA drone pilot certificate
(category A1/A3) in Europe.

## Overview

Getting a drone licence in Europe is unnecessarily hard. Official portals use
aviation jargon, documentation runs to dozens of pages, and commercial training
schools charge 700–800 EUR or more — even though the baseline A1/A3 certificate
requires only an online theory test, at no cost.

This project aims to fix that: a guided, visual, step-by-step preparation
platform that takes a complete beginner from zero to sitting the official exam.
Think Duolingo for drone regulation.

## Target certificate

### EASA A1/A3 (Open Category — Subcategory A1 and A3)

- Governed by EU regulation (EU) 2019/947
- Valid across the entire EASA zone (all EU member states)
- Obtained online, free of charge, through the national CAA portal
  (e.g. Latvia Drone Portal)
- Approximately 40 multiple-choice questions; ~75% passing score

## Planned MVP scope

- Interactive lessons on flight rules, restricted zones, safety, and privacy —
  no jargon, visuals first
- Practice tests based on real A1/A3 exam questions with per-question
  explanations
- Step-by-step registration guide for the national CAA portal
- Web-only; no native app for the initial release

## Certificate levels (product roadmap context)

| Level       | Target user              | Cost at CAA  | Complexity        |
|-------------|--------------------------|--------------|-------------------|
| A1/A3       | Recreational / light     | Free         | Online test only  |
| A2          | Larger, near people      | Partial fee  | Theory + self-dec |
| STS / BVLOS | Commercial operations    | 700–800 EUR+ | School + practical|

MVP focuses on A1/A3. A2 and STS preparation are post-MVP.
