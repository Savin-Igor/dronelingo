# Claims map — Post #2 Yellow / Orange drone alerts

Locked 2026-05-28. Sources visible on this date only.

## Hard facts (must appear in all three language versions, identical numbers/names)

1. NBS (Nacionālie bruņotie spēki, National Armed Forces) introduced a
   two-level cell-broadcast alert system. Live since **23 May 2026**.
2. Two levels: **Yellow** and **Orange**.
3. **Yellow** = alert that does *not* require immediate action; informs
   residents about a *possible* threat in Latvian airspace.
4. **Orange** = notification of a *detected* threat in Latvian airspace;
   requires immediate action per the instructions inside the message.
5. The alert level can move up or down (Yellow ↔ Orange) as the threat
   evolves.
6. On iOS devices, Orange is delivered as "Extreme Alert".
7. Orange message instructions to civilians:
   - Seek a safe place indoors.
   - Follow the **two-wall principle** (two solid walls between you and
     the outside).
   - If you see a low-flying suspicious object, **do not approach** it,
     call **112**.
   - Wait for the all-clear from NBS.
8. End-of-threat notification: NBS sends an explicit end-of-alert
   broadcast; further information published on social media account
   **"Latvijas armija"**.
9. Reference resources for civilians: **sargs.lv**, **112.lv**.
10. Named official: **Col. Arvis Zīle**, head of the Crisis Management
    Center, previously noted that Latvia already has up to **six**
    cell-broadcast message types — context-of-confusion risk.
11. Delivery channel: cell broadcast — no app needed, works on all
    carriers, language follows the device.

## Derived guidance (must be flagged as derivation, not as NBS instruction)

For a **drone pilot mid-flight**, paraphrased SOP:
- **Yellow received**: heightened awareness, finish current task with
  priority on safe landing path, be ready to abort. No legal obligation
  to land immediately, but operating environment has changed.
- **Orange received**: land or RTH immediately, log time + coordinates,
  shut down aircraft, follow the civilian instructions (two-wall rule),
  do not approach unknown objects, call 112 if you see one.
- Document in your flight log: which level received, when, your action.
  Useful if your operation later attracts CAA Latvia review.

This SOP is *our reading* for drone operators. The original NBS
guidance is written for the general public — it does not address pilots
specifically.
