# Claims map — Post #1 Rezekne incident: what changed for civilian pilots

Locked 2026-05-28. Sources visible on this date only. News is still
moving — do **not** speculate beyond what is sourced.

## Hard facts (must appear in all three language versions)

1. **7 May 2026**, early hours: three drones crossed into Latvian
   airspace from Russia. Two came down on Latvian territory; a third
   later exited.
2. One drone struck the (empty) tanks at a fuel depot in **Rezekne**,
   ~40 km into Latvian territory from the Russian border.
3. **4 empty oil tanks** were damaged. No casualties.
4. **French military aircraft** from NATO's Baltic Air Policing
   mission were scrambled during the alert.
5. Flight restrictions imposed up to ~**6 km altitude** in Latvia's
   eastern border region; lifted later that day.
6. On **10 May 2026**, Latvia confirmed the drones were **Ukrainian**.
   Ukraine's foreign minister linked the incident to Russian electronic
   warfare diverting Ukrainian drones from their targets.
7. **10 May 2026**: Latvia's Defence Minister **Andris Sprūds**
   announced his resignation, three days after the incident.
8. **14 May 2026**: Latvia's government collapsed. PM **Evika Siliņa**
   resigned.
9. **27 May 2026**: Latvian armed forces announced deployment of
   **mobile intercept teams** to the eastern border — 4 soldiers in
   4x4 vehicles equipped with interceptor drones from Latvian
   manufacturers **Origin Robotics** and **Eraser**. Goal: operational
   by early next month. Named source: Maj. **Modris Kairišs**, head of
   the Autonomous Systems Competence Center.
10. Eastern border with Russia and Belarus is **~400 km long**.
11. Independent Russian media outlet Novaya Gazeta Europe counted at
    least **24 drone incidents** in the three Baltic countries since
    early 2025.
12. Two drones from same Ukrainian campaign exploded at an empty oil
    storage facility in Latvia (the Rezekne event referenced above).
13. Latvia is seeking Ukrainian **expertise**, not hardware (tactical
    procedures, sensors, kill-chain).

## Pilot-facing derivations (must be flagged as our reading)

- **Latgale operating environment changed materially**. Daytime CAA
  authorization is harder to get; NOTAMs and TFRs may appear with very
  short notice; intercept teams now operate in the zone with their own
  drones.
- **Cross-border events trigger temporary altitude caps** (the 6 km
  cap on May 7 is precedent — your sub-120 m operation is well below
  that, but the airspace can close entirely).
- **Pre-flight checklist additions** post-Rezekne:
  - Re-check NOTAMs same morning (events can land overnight).
  - Verify your alert-broadcast capability ([[post-02-claims-map]] —
    Yellow/Orange).
  - Have an abort SOP for "civil ATC declares restriction in your
    sector mid-flight".
- **Do not approach unfamiliar wreckage**. The standard guidance for
  civilians (call 112, do not approach) also applies if you spot one
  while flying — land, log, call.
